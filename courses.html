Training Levels

Beginners Level: Adavus(Basic Steps in Bharatnatyam)

Tatta Adavu(Tapping the legs)

Natta Adavu(Stretching and synchronization of hands and legs)

Vishara Adavu(Swinging of hands in different directions i.e sideways, up and down keeping leg movement simple & straight in one line )

Panchajati or Tattimetti Adavu(Tap & jump on toes)

Kutta Adavu(Tap & raising heels)

Kuditta Mettadavu(Jumping on toes)

Sarikal Adavu(Sliding with samapadham, no Araimandi)

Korvai adavu(Sequence of all adavus)

Intermediate Level 1: (Advanced Adavus)

Mandi Adavu(Steps in Full mandi)

Paychal Adavu(Jumping on toes, advanced)

Sarikal Adavu(Sliding in advanced position)

Teermanam Adavu(Conclude the dance sequence)

Adavu Korvais in Adi & Roopaka Taalam

Alarippu (Tisra ekam)

Intermediate Level 2: (More Advanced)

Jatiswaram(Roopaka Taalam, Adavus performed to swara patterns)

Kavuthukam(Praise of Lord)

Natesha Kavuthuvam

Subramanya Kavuthuvam

Ganesha Kavuthuvam

Navasandhi Kavuthuvam

Kali Kavuthuvam

Pushpanjali(Offer flowers,music,love to Lord)

Expert Level

Padam(Narrative expressional Dance)

Shabdam(Interpretative narrative usually performed in seven beats)

Varnam(Elaborative descriptions of the nature of God)

Javali(usually feature the Nayika addressing her beloved, or the divine being, from a human level

Tillana(Performed at the end of performance & is a purely abstract form devoid of narrative)
